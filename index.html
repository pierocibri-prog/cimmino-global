The stuff nobody warns you about

You have a great product idea. You find suppliers online. And then reality hits, because sourcing from China is nothing like it looks from the outside.

01
You can't tell who's actually a factory
Most suppliers on public platforms are traders pretending to be manufacturers. They look the same, say the same things, and you have no way to know until the wrong shipment arrives at your door.
02
You're paying more than you should
Every middleman between you and the factory adds a margin. Two or three layers in and you're paying well above the real factory price, invisibly, every single order.
03
The details that kill your margins
Missing a certification? Your product gets blocked at customs. Wrong specs? You eat the cost. These aren't edge cases, they happen to almost everyone doing this for the first time.
04
Communication is harder than you think
Time zones, language barriers, early morning calls, and even then you're never quite sure if what was agreed is actually what's being made. That uncertainty is exhausting, and costly.
05
Minimum orders that don't match your reality
Factories want volume. You want to test first. That gap is where a lot of good ideas die, or where people over-commit and end up sitting on inventory they can't move.
