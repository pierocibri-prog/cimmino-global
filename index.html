The Challenge

What you're really up against

Sourcing from China is not a Google search. It is a complex operation with dozens of variables, each one capable of costing you time, money, or both.

01
You can't tell who's actually a factory
90% of suppliers on public platforms are traders posing as manufacturers. They look identical, say the same things, and without institutional verification, you're guessing, and guessing wrong is expensive.
02
You're paying margins you can't even see
Every layer between you and the factory takes a cut, quietly, invisibly. Two or three intermediaries and you're paying 30–60% above factory price without ever knowing it.
03
Certifications, compliance, regulations
CE, FCC, EN71, REACH, FDA, depending on your product and market, the compliance landscape is a minefield. One wrong move and your shipment is blocked at customs or pulled from shelves.
04
Language, culture, negotiation, it's a different world
Business in China operates on different rules, timelines, and expectations. Relationship and context matter more than contracts. What seems agreed often isn't.
05
Quality control and logistics don't end at the order
Getting the right factory is step one. What happens during production, before shipment, and at customs, that's where most projects fall apart.
